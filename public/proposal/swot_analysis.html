Future Stars Academy – SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Innovative project‑based learning model that integrates AI, engineering, and entrepreneurship.
  • Strong founder vision and personal investment (≈ M230,500 in assets, curriculum, IP).
  • Existing ICT infrastructure: 10 desktops, printer, office furniture.
  • Vocational equipment already in place (commercial oven, broiler).
  • Flexible delivery – after‑school, weekend, holiday camps, and online.
  • Innovation Passport creates a portable, skills‑based credential for learners.
  • Multiple diversified revenue streams planned.
  • Scalable business model with clear expansion pathways.

Weaknesses

  • New brand with limited market recognition and track record.
  • Limited seed capital; reliance on external funding for Phase 1.
  • Physical space constraints during the initial rollout.
  • Small core team wearing multiple hats.
  • Marketing budget is modest, reducing early outreach reach.
  • Digital platform still under development.
  • Dependence on grants and sponsorships in early years.

Opportunities

  • Rising demand for STEM, AI, and digital skills across Lesotho and the region.
  • Government priorities on youth employment, entrepreneurship, and digital transformation.
  • Potential strategic partnerships with ministries, BEDCO, universities, TVET institutes, and tech firms.
  • Expansion to adult learning, corporate training, and regional markets.
  • Commercialisation of student‑generated products and services.
  • Access to international donor funding for youth innovation and sustainable development.

Threats

  • Economic downturns could reduce household ability to pay fees.
  • Competition from existing tuition centres, coding schools, and emerging innovation hubs.
  • Rapid technology change requiring continual curriculum updates.
  • Shortage of qualified AI/engineering instructors.
  • Increasing operational costs (rent, utilities, internet).
  • Cybersecurity risks as digital platforms scale.
  • Potential shifts in donor funding priorities.
  • Regulatory changes affecting private education providers.
